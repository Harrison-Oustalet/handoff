## Transcript

**Ethan:** Alright everyone, thanks for joining. The goal today is to finalize priorities for the next-generation Apple Vision product launch and make sure every team is aligned before executive review next Wednesday. We’re behind in a few areas, especially software polish and supply chain timing, so we need concrete action items before the end of this meeting.

**Olivia:** Marketing has already drafted the initial teaser campaign. The current direction focuses heavily on immersive productivity and entertainment instead of just positioning the headset as futuristic hardware. We think the emotional angle performs better in testing.

**Ethan:** Good. When can we see finalized ad concepts?

**Olivia:** We can finish the full ad draft by Friday afternoon. We also need final hardware renders from design before export.

**Ava:** The design team can send updated renders by Thursday morning. We’re still adjusting the outer shell finish because fingerprints are more visible than expected under studio lighting.

**Marcus:** Does changing the finish impact production cost?

**Ava:** Slightly. About four dollars more per unit if we use the matte-coated aluminum option.

**Marcus:** Four dollars at scale is still significant. We need a comparison report before approving anything.

**Ethan:** Ava, can you prepare a side-by-side durability and cost presentation by Monday?

**Ava:** Yes.

**Daniel:** On the software side, eye tracking calibration is much smoother now, but hand gesture recognition still struggles in low-light environments.

**Jordan:** AI prediction models improved gesture accuracy by around 18% during internal testing, but latency increases when too many background processes run simultaneously.

**Ethan:** Can we optimize that before beta testing?

**Daniel:** Possibly, but we’d need another week from the systems team.

**Jordan:** We also need more real-world user testing data. Internal employees behave differently because they already know how the interface works.

**Nina:** Customer Experience can organize another round of public usability sessions. We should target first-time users instead of experienced testers.

**Ethan:** Make that happen. Schedule at least five usability sessions before next Thursday.

**Sarah:** Retail teams are asking when demo units will ship. Stores want enough time to train employees before launch week.

**Chris:** Shipping timelines depend on the manufacturing plant in Vietnam. The latest report says there may be delays with lens assembly components.

**Marcus:** How bad are we talking?

**Chris:** Worst-case scenario is a two-week delay if the supplier misses inspection standards again.

**Ethan:** That cannot happen during launch quarter. Chris, contact the supplier directly and get updated quality assurance reports by tomorrow morning.

**Chris:** Understood.

**Olivia:** Social media planning is moving fast. TikTok engagement predictions are stronger than Instagram right now, especially with short-form demos showing spatial computing features.

**Ethan:** I want teaser clips ready before competitors announce anything similar.

**Olivia:** The social team can prepare teaser posts by next Monday. We also want behind-the-scenes footage from the industrial design lab.

**Ava:** We’ll allow filming after the prototype cleanup is done.

**Marcus:** Finance still needs projected manufacturing totals. Right now we only have estimates from January.

**Chris:** Supply chain can update the forecast after we receive revised production numbers.

**Marcus:** I need finalized projections before the quarterly budgeting meeting on Tuesday.

**Chris:** Alright, I’ll prioritize it.

**Jordan:** Another thing — AI summarization inside the headset is working surprisingly well. Users can record meetings and automatically generate notes, reminders, and task lists.

**Daniel:** But memory usage spikes after transcripts exceed thirty minutes.

**Ethan:** Is it stable enough for keynote demonstrations?

**Daniel:** Yes, but barely. We should optimize memory management before public demos.

**Jordan:** I can assign two engineers to focus only on transcript compression and caching improvements this week.

**Ethan:** Do it.

**Sarah:** Retail also requested updated training materials. Current onboarding videos still use outdated interface screenshots from January.

**Daniel:** That’s because the UI changed again last week.

**Sarah:** Then we need final screenshots locked before training starts.

**Ethan:** Software team needs to freeze the onboarding UI by Friday evening.

**Daniel:** We can commit to that if no additional design changes are requested afterward.

**Ava:** Fair enough.

**Nina:** Customer testers consistently mention headset weight during long sessions. Most users love the display quality, but neck fatigue becomes noticeable after forty minutes.

**Ava:** The hardware team is already testing lighter headband materials.

**Marcus:** Does that affect manufacturing schedules?

**Ava:** Slightly, but not enough to impact launch if decisions are made this week.

**Ethan:** Then prioritize comfort improvements. We cannot ignore repeated customer feedback.

**Chris:** One more supply chain issue — shipping costs increased nearly 11% this quarter.

**Marcus:** We expected increases, but not that high.

**Chris:** Air freight is the problem. Sea shipping would reduce costs but increase delivery times.

**Sarah:** Retail launch timing matters more than minor shipping savings.

**Marcus:** Agreed. We stay with air freight for launch inventory.

**Olivia:** PR also wants executive talking points prepared before media interviews begin.

**Ethan:** I’ll handle keynote direction, but marketing should prepare supporting documents.

**Olivia:** We’ll draft executive speaking notes by Tuesday afternoon.

**Jordan:** AI voice interaction still needs work. Users occasionally trigger commands accidentally during conversations nearby.

**Daniel:** We can reduce sensitivity thresholds, but then voice activation feels less responsive.

**Ethan:** Prioritize reducing accidental triggers. Bad demos spread online instantly.

**Ethan:** Alright, quick recap before we close:
- Marketing finishes ad draft by Friday

Anything else before we wrap?

**Sarah:** Retail still needs confirmed shipment quantities for flagship stores.

**Chris:** I’ll send estimates by Monday morning.

**Ethan:** Perfect. Thanks everyone. Let’s stay aggressive on timelines because executive review is approaching fast.
